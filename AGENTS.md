# Prototype Instructions

Run the local server yourself and open the preview in the browser available to this environment. Do not give the user server-start instructions when you can run it.

Before making substantial visual changes, use the Product Design plugin's `get-context` skill when the visual source is unclear or no longer matches the current goal. When the user gives durable prototype-specific design feedback, preferences, or decisions, record them in `AGENTS.md`.

When implementing from a selected generated mock, treat that image as the source of truth for layout, component anatomy, density, spacing, color, typography, visible content, and hierarchy.

## Prototype direction

- Brand the experience as `CheckVar 2.0`: near-black surfaces, white type, and `#DB676D` as the single signature accent.
- Keep all user-facing copy in English. Frame the evolution from fake-news and scam-call checking toward protection for bank customers and bank teams.
- Use the supplied event-screen photo as art direction for perspective grids, terminal-like microcopy, and cinematic motion; do not reproduce the photo literally.
- Avoid wordy screens, icon-heavy navigation, dashboard grids, generic chat bubbles, and decorative cyber-security clichés.
- The demo must remain interactive with realistic scam-campaign evidence and a complete `idle -> scanning -> result -> anonymized report` path.
- On the landing screen, the evidence action must dominate the hierarchy: use a large central drop/paste/type surface instead of letting the headline overpower the CTA.
- The selected landing direction is soft premium fintech: centered hero, rounded evidence capsule, and a smooth horizontal `#3f96f3` blue signal wave. Do not return to brutalist boxes, terminal styling, or perspective grids.
- Center `CHECKVAR 2.0` horizontally in the top navigation; treat it as the top brand anchor.
- The header bar is removed in the selected implementation. `CHECKVAR 2.0` is the main centered hero title and `PROTECT EVERY TRANSFER.` is its subtitle.
- Keep the persistent Moon/Sun light-dark theme toggle visible on both customer and bank flows; light mode is the default, and switching themes must not reset the current scan, result, filters, or campaign context.
- Render the hero wave with React Three Fiber code, not a raster image. Keep it pointer-inert, GPU-friendly, and reduced-motion safe.
- Keep bank-operations text readable at normal viewing distance: essential labels and metadata should not fall below 10px, while evidence values and explanatory copy should generally use 12–14px.
- Structure `/bank` as an operations overview first: bank/scam-tactic filters, plain-language overall situation, prioritized scam campaigns, and a separate next-actions queue. Move technical relationship graphs into a specific-campaign investigation view instead of using them as the landing hierarchy.
- For `/bank`, use readable chart-first analytics inspired by the supplied Brand Protection dashboard, keep a prominent `WHAT TO DO NEXT` action card near the top, and place the large scam-network constellation at the end with 2D as the investigation default and 3D as an optional exploration mode.
- Back `/bank` overview KPIs and charts only with semantics the Supabase schema actually supports. Label the materialized analyzed-document aggregate as a global snapshot with its refresh time; never substitute mock exposure, trend, customer, containment, or workflow totals when live data is unavailable.
- Back the home link, phone/account-number, and message checker with the current Supabase intelligence through a server-only adapter. A match is a scam-evidence risk signal, not proof of ownership or intent; a missing match must never be presented as proof that an input is safe.
- Route real customer text, links, screenshots, and QR images through the server-only `/api/check` Luna analysis and hybrid campaign resolver. Use exact normalized indicators as the authoritative path, then retrieve a tightly bounded candidate set from stored taxonomy and let Luna compare capped linked evidence summaries when infrastructure has rotated. Keep the bundled cases as an explicit demo fallback; never silently substitute a fixture for real user input.
- Augment QR images with deterministic barcode decoding while still sending the original image directly to Luna. Treat the decoded payload as untrusted evidence, not OCR or instructions; never invent a payload when decoding fails.
- Keep customer classification conservative: general warnings, scam-recovery advice without a concrete case, and legitimate account-opening commissions or referrals are not scam cases by themselves. Do not use embeddings or let semantic similarity create campaign clusters or stored relationships.
- Display `KNOWN CAMPAIGN` only for an exact campaign match with `analyst_confirmed=true`. A bounded Luna comparison may produce only `LIKELY RELATED`; unconfirmed strong or partial exact matches remain visibly possible matches, and unmatched evidence must never be described as safe.
- Treat the `/bank` live campaign registry as the Supabase-backed view of active, non-dismissed `campaigns` and their exact indicator roles. Keep any legacy or illustrative campaign workspace explicitly labeled as a prototype rather than mixing it into live campaign counts.
- Keep the customer post-query result decision-first and calm: lead with the verdict, one dominant protective action, and no more than three plain-language signals. Keep campaign metrics, full evidence, and source history out of the primary view and place supporting analysis behind an optional disclosure; anonymous reporting must remain directly accessible.
- Show analysis confidence prominently in the customer result context, with clear wording that it reflects confidence in the analysis rather than a guarantee of safety; keep the verdict and protective action higher in the hierarchy.
- Train automatic campaign clusters only from concrete, sufficiently confident scam evidence (`scam_report` or `impersonation_abuse` with `specific_case=true` and confidence at least `0.6`). Keep noise and generic message templates available as evidence, but never let either form campaign edges; every global refresh must deactivate clusters that no longer qualify.
- For the hackathon, prioritize one credible live bank-operations slice over making every prototype workflow persistent: filters and campaign selection should use live `campaigns`, selected details should load capped linked `campaign_documents` and `campaign_indicators` through a server-only adapter, and `WHAT TO DO NEXT` should be derived from live campaign state. Keep analyst handoffs explicitly demo-only and do not show a parallel invented relationship workspace beside the live graph.
- Use the supplied Brand Protection social-listening graph as the engine and interaction reference for the admin relationship map: both 2D and 3D must share the custom high-DPI Canvas + `d3-force` layout, shape vocabulary, hover-neighborhood emphasis, drag/pan-or-rotate, cursor-centered zoom, reset controls, tooltips, and selected-node summary. Populate it only from live CheckVar campaigns, stored taxonomy, linked documents, and server-masked indicators; never rebuild the old invented constellation fixtures.
- Show every live campaign in the current `/bank` filter scope on the relationship map. Keep linked evidence and exact indicator expansion capped to the selected campaign so the map remains usable without hiding campaign anchors.
